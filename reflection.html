Personal Reflection

What I’ve Learned and Where I’ll Go

My journey in computer science has been shaped by academic challenges and hands-on experience that strengthened my foundation. These challenges and experiences pushed me to continue earning my degree while building mental fortitude and a greater understanding of technology.

Balancing professional software engineering work with my degree allowed me to confidently apply theory to practice. This also allowed me to develop time management skills and networking experience while still in the earliest stages of my career.

Brazilian Jiu Jitsu has taught me discipline, resilience, and problem-solving under pressure, skills that directly influence how I approach complex software challenges. These practical skills are priceless and motivate me to continue honing my craft professionally as well as through my hobbies.

My long-term goal is to grow as an engineer while contributing to impactful projects that solve real problems. I want to enjoy the work that I do and leverage the skills I have built so far to help people. With shifting times, software might not be the focus of many careers, so I intend to take these skills into whatever job I decide to pursue.
